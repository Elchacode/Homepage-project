About Awo-Idemili

Awo-Idemili is an enviable place of culture and tradition.
a town deeply rooted in its rich history and vibrant customs,
making it a unique and fascinating destination for visitors and a cherished home for its people.

Christ the King Catholic (CKC) Church

Established in 1966, Christ the King Catholic (CKC) Church
holds the distinction of being the oldest church in Orsu Local Government Area.
As a cornerstone of spiritual life for the community,
CKC Church has been a beacon of faith and a gathering place for generations.
Its long-standing history is a testament to the deep-rooted religious traditions that have shaped the region.

Awo Idemili Palm Plantation

Awo Idemili Palm Plantation is renowned as the largest palm oil plantation in the entire region.
Its vast expanse of palm trees and efficient production processes make it a leader in palm oil export.
This plantation plays a crucial role in the local economy, contributing the highest volume of palm oil exports in the region.
Its significance extends beyond agriculture, serving as a symbol of economic strength and sustainability for the community.

Awo Idemili Waterfall

Awo Idemili Waterfall is one of the most breathtaking natural landmarks in the region.
Known for its cascading waters and serene environment, it attracts both locals and tourists alike.
The waterfall is not only a scenic destination but also a key part of the area's natural ecosystem, supporting various plant and animal species.
Visitors often come to enjoy the tranquil atmosphere, making it a popular spot for relaxation and picnics.
